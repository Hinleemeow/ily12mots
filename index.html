☁ Our little timeline

12 months of choosing you

We may not have photos together yet, so I made these little memory cards instead. Every month still meant something to me.

1

The Beginning

Before I met you, I was not looking for someone to love. I was only looking for someone I could be comfortable with, a companion, a friend, someone I could play games with and share little moments with.

Meeting you felt expected because you came into my life naturally, but falling in love with you was never part of my plan. It just happened, ILY. You slowly became someone I did not want to lose, not because you were simply there, but because being with you felt different, soft, and real.

2

Getting Closer

Little talks, laughs, mga tawanan natin, and mga kulitan slowly became special to me. I started feeling safer because you were there, BIII, waiting for me, caring for me, and making my days feel less heavy.

Without forcing anything, you became someone I wanted to talk to, someone I looked for, and someone I felt at peace with. You became part of my routine, but more than that, MISIS, you became part of my heart.

3

Soft Feelings

At first, maybe you fell first, but I think I fell harder. I started realizing that this was not just a simple crush anymore. You became someone I cared about in a way I could not ignore.

I missed you every day, ILY, even when I was busy, even when I had work, even when I tried to focus on other things. Somehow, you became my rest. After a long day, ikaw yung gusto kong balikan. You became my soft place in a world that often felt tiring.

4

Still Choosing

That month was not easy for us, BIII. We had fights, misunderstandings, and moments where our feelings were tested. There were times when we hurt each other without meaning to, and times when pride made things heavier than they should have been.

But even through all of that, we still chose to grow. We chose to learn, to forgive, and to understand each other better. It was not a perfect month, MISIS, but it proved something important: even when things were hard, we still chose each other.

5

The Game of Trust

Our fifth month was probably one of the hardest challenges we faced. It felt like everything became a test of trust. May nalaman ako, may mga sinabi, may mga evidence, and honestly, it was hard to know what to believe at first.

But even with all the doubts and confusion, I chose to believe you, ILY. I chose to trust your words. I chose to stay, not because it was easy, but because my heart still wanted to believe in you and in us.

6

Halfway There

Six months, BIII. Half a year. Ang dami na nating pinagdaanan by that time. Ang daming fights, ang daming paulit-ulit na misunderstandings, may napapagod, may sumusuko, may nasasaktan, pero somehow, nandito pa rin tayo.

It was not easy, and I will never pretend that it was. But maybe that is what made it emotional. We kept trying even when we were tired. We kept holding on even when things felt heavy. Six months passed, MISIS, and despite everything, we were still making it.

7

The Hardest Silence

Our seventh month was painful. We entered that phase of no contact for a month and one week, and honestly, it was hard. Sobra. Little by little, I felt myself letting go. Unti-unti nagbu-build up yung tampo, unti-unti napapagod yung puso ko, and unti-unti parang nawawala yung love.

Kaya ko sanang bumitaw, ILY. But I did not. I still chose to stay, even when it hurt. And I know it was not only hard for me. It was hard for you too. We both suffered in our own ways, BIII, but even in that silence, something in me still hoped for us.

8

Quiet Proof

After the no-contact phase, we finally found our way back to each other. Hindi agad naging okay lahat. May tampo pa rin sa puso ko, may walls pa rin akong binuo, and I was still scared to fully let everything feel normal again.

But slowly, MISIS, those walls started to go down. Slowly, the pain became lighter. Slowly, I remembered why I kept choosing you. Love is not always loud. Sometimes it is patience. Sometimes it is healing. Sometimes it is choosing the same person again, even after the hardest silence.

9

Still You

By the ninth month, I think we already knew that loving each other was not always going to be easy. We had different moods, different thoughts, different fears, and sometimes we did not know how to handle each other properly.

But even with all of that, ILY, the answer stayed the same. It was still you. Kahit ang daming nangyari, kahit minsan ang gulo natin, kahit minsan hindi natin alam paano aayusin, ikaw pa rin yung gusto kong intindihin. Ikaw pa rin yung gusto kong piliin.

10

Learning to Communicate

Our tenth month taught me that love is not only about staying. It is also about learning how to communicate, how to listen, and how to say what hurts before it becomes too heavy.

We kept saying that we needed to communicate, BIII, but sometimes pride got in the way. Walang nagpapatalo, nagpapataasan tayo ng pride, and without noticing it, nasasaktan na natin yung isa’t isa. But I think that month showed us that love also needs honesty, not just feelings.

11

The Small Things

Our eleventh month made me realize that sometimes, it is not always the big fights that hurt the most. Sometimes, it is the small things. The little misunderstandings, the unsaid feelings, the tiny triggers, and the things we kept inside because we did not know how to open up properly.

Those small things can fill the cup until it spills, ILY. And maybe that is what happened to us sometimes. But even then, MISIS, I still wanted us to learn. I still wanted us to understand each other better. I still wanted us to choose softness instead of pride.

12

Today

Twelve months since I started courting you, BIII. Twelve months of patience, feelings, fights, silence, trust, forgiveness, and choosing you again and again.

Hindi naging madali yung story natin, ILY. We were not always gentle. We were not always okay. We had moments where we almost lost each other, moments where we got tired, and moments where love felt heavy. But look at us now, still here, still trying, still holding on to what we have.

And honestly, MISIS, if I could go back to the beginning, I would still choose to meet you. I would still choose to know you. I would still choose to love you.
